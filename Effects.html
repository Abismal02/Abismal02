What are the lasting effects of
The Berlin Wall?

After The Berlin Wall was taken down the reunification process
began, there was no more east and west, but rather one whole
state. Germany slowly started to govern itsef and recover from
the massive divide within their own country. However, today,
more than thrity years later, the divide in germany is still very
prominent. When the wall originally fell Germany started to
tax the richer members of society in order to help rebuild and
unify, those taxes are still in place today. Due to the rather
poor effort to rebuild the east the younger generations moved
west even after the reunification in hopes of a better life.
The charts on the left represent such, showing income, age,
and unemployment, almost perfectly divided by what used to be
the iron curtain. In Germany today the affects of the wall are
felt not only on a chart, but also by the locals. Many people
feel that when the wall came down there was an adjustment, and
a very onesided one at that. People, almost unanimously, agree
that the eastern Germans had a much harder time adjusting, and
thus took second priority to west Germany, advancing without them.
In addition, there is still a communist connotation on east Germany.
In early 2023 Mathias Döpfner, the CEO of Europes largest media
publishing company, stated that all east Germans are communists:
“The ossis [east Germans] are either communists or fascists. They
don’t do in-between. Disgusting" (The Guardian). Although Döpfner
was ridiculed for saying such, it shows that despite the reunification
efforts, Germany is just as divided as it was sixty-two years ago.
